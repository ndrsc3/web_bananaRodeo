Code of Conduct

Stay cool as a banana!

ACCOUNTABILITY

Our Sponsorship Model

Our community expands via a sponsorship model, where current members in good standing can invite friends to our event. When you invite someone, you are endorsing them and taking on the responsibility of integrating them into both the event and our community.

After your friend attends one of our events, they gain the ability to invite others. Therefore, select your guests carefully; you're not just inviting a friend, but also potentially opening the door to their network of friends.

Can I invite people?

If you received an email invitation directly from Banana Rodeo, you are welcome to invite friends. Please use your discretion and good judgment—invite only those who you believe will blend well with our existing community.

However, if a friend invited you and not Banana Rodeo directly, you cannot invite your friends just yet. We will add you to the invitation list once you've attended one of our events, but we ask that you experience our community before contributing to its growth.

RESPONSIBILITY

You are responsible for your friends

If your friend causes an issue, we will contact you for assistance in resolving it. Please refrain from inviting friends who might cause disruptions at our private event. If your invitee breaches our community agreements, your invitation privileges may be suspended or revoked.

How can I set up my invited friends for success?

Only community members who have previously attended our events or have been invited by long-standing community members are on our mailing list. If your first-time guest RSVP'd for an upcoming private event using their own email address, they will receive our logistical messages for that event. After they have successfully attended their first event, they will be added to our main mailing list.

Please encourage your invited friends to frequent our website for all information about this private event.

Be prepared for day-of

Please ensure your invited friends are prepared for the experience they are about to have. Make sure they bring everything they need and brief them on the ground rules:
  • Never post party details online
  • No media on the day of the event
  • No location tagging on social media
  • Always obtain permission from everyone in a photograph before posting it online
  • Smoke only in designated areas
  • No candles or fires without permission
  • No firearms or weapons
  • Follow consent rules
  • No persons under the age of 21 without a chaperone

How to sponsor someone

When you receive your invitation to this party, you will be able to RSVP. This is a requirement for attending the private event.

Once your invitation has been RSVP'd, you will have the opportunity to email one (1) person an invitation to this event.

SAFETY

It's a community effort

All attendees play a role in ensuring community safety at The Banana Rodeo. Remember to:

  • Stay hydrated
  • Wear sunscreen
  • Eat
  • Rest
  • Set clear boundaries
  • Respect those around you

Team up with a friend, look out for one another, and pace yourself. Practice good self-care.

Be kind and self-aware. Take care of your environment.

Banana Rodeo attendees are supported by the Banana Business Bureau (BBB), which helps create and maintain our safe and fun environment. BBB members and volunteers are available to assist with safety concerns at any time.

MEDIA POLICY

The Media Policy aims to:

  • Protect the privacy and consent of guests, performers, and volunteers
  • Ensure the sustainability of the Banana Rodeo by preventing media exposure and uninvited guests from discovering or attending our private event

We believe this policy is essential for the longevity of our community. To achieve this, it's crucial that our guests understand these guidelines and support each other in following them.

If you're ever unsure about what is or isn't allowed under this policy, keep this overview in mind and use discretion to maintain the privacy of the event and its guests.

All GUESTS must adhere to these rules regarding any content (photos, videos, and audio) captured at The Banana Rodeo:

NO IDENTIFYING INFORMATION

No identifying information about the private event (such as the production company name, event name, location, date, etc.) should be posted anywhere.

No one should be able to discover Banana Rodeo online, in a publication, or in a public presentation, or attend an event without an invitation.

Identifying information includes, but is not limited to:

  • Banana Rodeo name, with the exception of nicknames (Book Club, Granny's Funeral, Egg House)
  • The Banana Rodeo name can be shared for professional use under certain circumstances (please contact the BBB directly regarding Professional Use of Media by Artists for more information)
  • Details and names of projects, other than your own, without permission
  • Geolocation and location tagging

KEEP DISCRETION IN MIND AT ALL TIMES

When posting and interacting on social media, please refer to our Sponsorship Model and Code of Conduct. Keep in mind that there will most likely be folks who are not sponsees viewing your content – and they may not be folks you want to be responsible for!

We ask that if you do post on social media, please lean on nicknames and discretion at all times and target your content towards private audiences.

Acceptable Private Platforms include:

  • Private social media posts (Instagram stories/posts set to "Close Friends", private Instagram accounts, etc.)
  • Signal Group Chats

UNACCEPTABLE Platforms include, but are not limited to:

  • Public social media posts (Facebook posts set to "friends of friends" or less private, public IG accounts)
  • Blog posts
  • Podcasts
  • Personal websites (for artists and other professional portfolios, please contact the BBB for more information)
  • Articles in print or web formats, as well as any publicly accessible websites or printed materials
  • Public presentations

RECEIVE CONSENT BEFORE CAPTURING & POSTING ANY MEDIA

Exercise discretion when using cameras at our private event. Respect that not everyone wishes to be photographed or filmed, receiving consent can be difficult, and the presence of cameras without consent can alter the atmosphere for those around.

Polaroid cameras and reusable film cameras offer a good substitute for cell phones. If you choose to capture media at The Banana Rodeo, please adhere to these guidelines:

  • Seek explicit verbal consent from individuals before capturing or sharing content
  • If you are unsure if your actions adhere to the guidelines, don't do it

For any additional questions or concerns regarding these guidelines, as well as more information regarding particular instances, please contact the BBB directly.
