# Banana Confessional

An interactive page built around the real-world Banana Confessional booth — a physical confessional set up at the event where attendees record audio confessions. Years of recordings have been archived.

## Concept

Bring the confessional archive online in a fun, creative, web1.0-flavored experience. The site should feel like entering a confessional — intimate, slightly absurd, and distinctly Banana Rodeo. Visitors can listen to submitted confessions and potentially submit their own.

## Key Questions

- How is the audio archive hosted? (file storage, S3/CDN, or Vercel Blob?)
- Can visitors submit new confessions online, or is it listen-only?
- How are confessions surfaced — random playback, browse list, sequential?
- What's the UI metaphor? An actual confessional booth? A cassette jukebox? A hotline?
- Moderation and privacy: are confessions anonymous? Can submitters request removal?
- Are all years of recordings available or just a curated selection?
- What makes this feel like the event vs. a generic audio player?

## Rough UX / Notes

- Leaning into the booth aesthetic: dark, candlelit, wooden paneling, a slot or window
- Could use a "push to confess" button for drama
- Playback with minimal UI — let the audio be the focus
- Web1.0 elements: blinking "CONFESSING..." indicator, retro equalizer bars
